About this Project :-

In modern organizations, understanding employee behavior and attrition patterns is essential for improving workforce management. The HR Analytics Dashboard is designed to analyze employee attrition, workforce demographics, salary distribution, and department-level performance.

This dashboard converts raw HR data into interactive insights that help HR teams identify key factors influencing employee turnover and develop strategies to improve retention and workforce planning.

Key Insights :-
  1. The organization has 1470 total employees, with 237 employees leaving the company, resulting in an attrition rate of around 16%.
  2. Employees aged 26–35 contribute the highest attrition, indicating early-career professionals are more likely to leave.
  3. Laboratory Technician, Sales Executive, and Research Scientist roles show higher attrition levels compared to other job roles.
  4. Employees with lower salary bands show higher attrition rates, suggesting compensation plays a key role in retention.
  5. Sales and Research departments contribute the highest employee turnover.
  6. Employees with shorter tenure (1–3 years) show significantly higher attrition compared to long-term employees.
Business Recommendation :-
  1. Improve employee engagement and career growth opportunities for employees in early career stages.
  2. Review salary structures and compensation policies for lower salary bands to improve retention.
  3. Implement targeted retention strategies for high attrition roles such as Sales Executives and Laboratory Technicians.
  4. Strengthen onboarding and development programs for employees within their first three years at the company.
